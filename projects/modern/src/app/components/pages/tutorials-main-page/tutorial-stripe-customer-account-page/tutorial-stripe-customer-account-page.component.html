Using Stripe Customer Account

Carry also uses the Customers feature from Stripe to manage paying for delivery listings and membership subscriptions. After creating an account, the platform automatically created a customer account for you.

On the settings screen, scroll down until you get to the "Stripe Customer Account" section.

This is where you can add any card information to associate to your Stripe customer account. Again, this is managed by Stripe; Carry does not keep or know about any personal finance data. For those who are more familiar with the tech side of things, the mobile app for Carry was developed using frameworks called React Native and Expo. Stripe created SDKs (software development kit) for other platforms to use their services, one of which is the Stripe React Native SDK. By using Stripe's SDK (and verification of Apple App Store/Google Play Store for compliance), user input is protected and secured.

After successfully submitting card information, you will see it as a payment option on your settings page.

With payment methods attached to your Stripe customer account, you can now pay for things on the platform, the 2 main things being listings and subscriptions. To enter a subscription with the platform, go to the "Membership" section, choose a payment method on file and tap "Subscribe".

The biggest perk of a membership with the platform is waived application fees:
As a customer, this means cheaper costs for creating delivery listings.
As a service provider, this means getting the whole payout of a delivery listing.

The "Membership" section will now show when the subscription renewal date is.

To cancel the subscription, tap "Cancel Subscription". The form will return to subscribe again however your canceled scription is still active until the original renewal date.
